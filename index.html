Short Bio
Hi! I'm a 2nd-year M.S. at University of Virginia, now working in the Vision, Language, and Learning Lab, supervised by Prof. Vicente Ordonez-Roman. I am also fortunate to have an internship with Prof. Hongning Wang. Prior to joining in UVa, I obtained my bachelor degree from the Beijing University of Posts and Telecommunications, where I worked with Prof. Chuang Zhang.

I have a broad interest in natural language processing, computer vision, and deep learning problems. My research goal is to make machine learn language from various contexts in the world. Currently, I evaluate this goal by multimodal tasks, such as image captions, multimodel summarization.
Publications&Manuscripts
  1. VisualNews: Benchmark and Challenges in Entity-aware Image Captioning
    Fuxiao Liu, Yinghan Wang, Tianlu Wang, Vicente Ordonez
    ArXiv, 2020 [paper] [code] [bibtex]
    Keywords: Image Caption, Visual Language, Natural Language Processing
    Descriptions: Introduced VisualNews, the largest and most diverse news image captioning dataset. Proposed VisualNews-Captioner, increasing CIDEr by 10+ points with fewer parameters than competing methods.
  2. MT: Multi-Modal Transformer for News Image Caption
    Fuxiao Liu, Yinghan Wang, Tianlu Wang, Vicente Ordonez
    Under Review, 2020 [paper] [code]
    Keywords: Vision and Language, Transformer
    Descriptions: Proposed the Multi-Modal Transformer, addressing two limitations of previous news caption models. Achieved state-of-art result on two datasets.
  3. The Research of Semantic Segmentation with Light Neural Networks
    Fuxiao Liu, Ming Wu, Chuang Zhang
    Bachelor Thesis, 2019 [paper]
    Keywords: Computer Vision, Semantic Segmentation, Encoder-Decoder and Lightweight
    Descriptions: Developed a novel lightweight Fully Convolutional Network, which achieved better accuracy with much smaller model size(17.2M) than baseline models(100+ M) on two Remote Sensing Datasets.
  4. Wait! An Embarrassing Look into Personalized Explanation for Recommendation
    Advisor: Prof. Hongning Wang, 2020 [paper] [code]
    Keywords: Explainable Recommendation, Natural Language Processing
    Descriptions: Extracted high-quality feature nouns from reviews on Yelp and Amazon datasets. Experiments with both personalized and non-personalized explainable recommendation models. Discovered that the non-personalized methods can perform better than personalized methods.
